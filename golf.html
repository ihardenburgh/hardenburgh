Wrist Supination at Impact 
Release 
• As Ben Hogan suggests, experiment with wrist supination through impact to square up clubface.
• Take note of wrist position when have adddressed the ball at setup...
    - With a stronger grip, the logo on your glove is pointing right of target.
    - Think about supinating wrist so much that you return this wrist to the exact position at impact.

Hands Low to Get the Club Up 
Takeaway 

...Notice how even though there is a slight bend to Adam Scott's trail arm, the upper portion of the trail arm forms an angle perpendicular to the ground, which establishes how close to the ball you should be.! This may cause a slight push to ball flight, but standing closer to ball so much that upper trail arm is perpendicular can prove to yield tighter dispersions. A potential way to fight a push can be by moving the ball up in your stance and aligning the clubhead a bit behind the ball to promote a closed face just like John Rahm does with his driver!
Quick Hands Through the Release 
Release 
Need to have quick hands through the release as having too slow hands will not allow the club face to square up and could even result in a hosel shot!
Wrist Hinge During Takeaway Into Set Position 
Takeaway; Set 

• Note how taking away the club with the shoulders and wrist hinge is a blend to the 1st parallel (i.e., sticks at 45° were just for demonstration purposes)!
• Perhaps just as importantly, don't try to manipulate the wrist into the set position, but simply turn into it and just apply more wrist hinge until the club is about shoulder high!
• If struggling to set club in a good position, as a drill, looking back during backswing seems to get everything in good form.
    - Do this until you feel comfortable taking a real shot to see if still maintaining that good form on camera.
    - Go back and forth between looking and not looking as needed.
Tommy Fleetwood's Short Follow-Through 
Follow-Through 

...Practice Tommy's sawed-off finish where shaft is almost perpendicular to the ground, as well as set up to ball with slightly negative face-to-path! Moreover, experiment with taking the club straight back on target line as opposed to more inside for a tighter club path!
Keeping Trail Elbow Close to Body and Not Too Deep? 
Takeaway; Transition 

• Need to keep trail elbow closer to body in backswing and more forward to prevent cupping of wrists and casting!
• To get this feeling as to how the trail elbow should work in your backswing in a drill, put your lead hand across your chest and under your trail elbow as you take an imaginary club back.
    - Notice how the lead hand prevents your trail elbow from going too deep and becoming disconnected!

High & Forward Hands When Putting
Putting 
• Keeping your hands higher in your grip helps to minimize wrist action and unnecessary face rotation during the stroke.
• Moving your hands slightly forward when putting, also known as a "forward press", can help promote a smoother stroke by minimizing wrist action and ensuring a square clubface.
Push Elbows Out When Putting
Putting 
• Prior to putting stroke, as part of pre-shot routine, push the elbows out away from your body, so much that the arms, shoulders and upper chest form a box.
• This help the arms and shoulders to move like a pendulum and helps to keep the head of the club from shaking.
